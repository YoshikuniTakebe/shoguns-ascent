# Monster reserve tracking and Luna no-space popup

Luna's monster purchase flow now detects when no valid province exists and shows a dedicated "no room" popup instead of the generic placement UI. The monster reserve counter (`player.monsters`) is incremented on four paths: Luna no-space, battle kills (seppuku and defeat), and betray. The sidebar reserve display switches from a single number to an X/Y format (in-reserve/total-owned), with dual-type monsters (Komainu, Hotei as shinto; Yurei, Fukurokuju as daimyo) contributing to both their monster count and their secondary type when in reserve.

**Watch for:** Komainu placed at a temple won't be detected as "deployed" by the reserve display logic, causing it to be counted as still in reserve (confirmed). The `useMemo` in `PlayerReserves` depends on the entire `gameState` object, making the memoization ineffective (confirmed). Fukurokuju's card text says "Counts as Daimyo and Fortress" but the dual-type display only accounts for the daimyo aspect, not fortress (confirmed — matches user spec but diverges from card rules).

## High-level view

The Luna no-space detection splits the former "skip and advance" inline block into two steps: increment the reserve and show a popup, then on dismiss advance the train. This separates the user-facing acknowledgment from the state machine progression.

Reserve tracking hooks into three return-to-reserve paths in `gameLogic.ts` (seppuku, battle defeat, betray) by adding `monsters += 1` branches alongside the existing bushi/shinto/daimyo handling. The seppuku branch also widens its figure filter to include monsters, which means monsters now die during seppuku — a gameplay behavior change, not just a tracking fix.

The `computeReserveTotals` function in PlayerPanel derives display numbers by scanning all map figures and cross-referencing deployed monster card IDs against the player's season cards. It has a gap: Komainu sent to worship at a temple creates a temple figure with only `{ playerId, figureId }` — no `monsterCardId` — so the function can't detect it as deployed and will double-count it.

## Komainu temple deployment detection gap

When `doKomainuPlaceAtTemple` fires, it pushes `{ playerId, figureId }` into the temple's figures array. No `monsterCardId` is stored. In `computeReserveTotals`, the deployed-monster detection only looks at map figures:

```typescript
allMapFigures.forEach(f => {
  if (f.type === 'monster' && f.monsterCardId) deployedMonsterCardIds.add(f.monsterCardId);
});
```

The subsequent `monsterCardsInReserve` filter finds all cards not in `deployedMonsterCardIds`, so a temple-deployed Komainu's card (`sp-komainu`) passes through. This means `monsterCardsInReserve` will include Komainu, triggering `shintoMonstersInReserve` to count it, adding +1 to effective shinto reserve and shinto total even though the figure is actively at a shrine.

The dead `allTempleFigures.forEach` loop with only comments suggests the author noticed this gap but didn't resolve it. A fix would require either storing the `monsterCardId` on the temple figure or maintaining a separate mapping of card-to-figureId when Komainu is sent to pray.

## Seppuku now includes monsters

The seppuku branch in `resolveNextBattle` previously only gathered bushi and daimyo figures. It now also selects monster figures for ritual suicide:

```typescript
const ownFigures = currentProvFigures.figures.filter(
  (f) => f.owner === highestBidder && (f.type === 'bushi' || f.type === 'daimyo' || f.type === 'monster')
);
```

Each killed monster increments `bidder.monsters += 1`, which is correct for reserve tracking. But the broader implication is that monsters are now killed during seppuku (granting VP and honor per monster killed). If monsters were previously exempt from seppuku by game design, this is a gameplay mechanics change that goes beyond tracking fixes. Worth confirming this is intended.
